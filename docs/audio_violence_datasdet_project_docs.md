AVDP Recording & Tagging Field Specification

1. Signal Acquisition & Technical Standards

All audio captured for the Audio Violence Dataset Project (AVDP) must adhere to these lossless standards to ensure compatibility with Audio Spectrogram Transformer (AST) and CNN architectures.

Environment & Hardware Requirements

SNR Monitoring: Actively monitor the Signal-to-Noise Ratio. If ambient noise (traffic, AC, electrical hum) causes SNR to drop below 15 dB, adjust microphone placement or apply portable acoustic shielding.

Microphone Technique: Maintain consistent distance and orientation. Use directional microphones for speech isolation and ambient mics for contextual environmental cues.

Silence Padding: Ensure at least 0.5s of "digital silence" or ambient baseline before and after target speech segments.

2. Administrative & File Management

Failure to follow naming conventions will result in ingestion errors within Linux-based training pipelines.

Filenames: ASCII characters only. No spaces, special characters, or UTF-8 characters above U+00A1.

Directory Structure: data/{Language}/{Speaker_ID}/{audio_file_name}.wav

Pairing: Every .wav file must have a corresponding .txt transcript file with the identical name in the same folder.

Redundancy: Avoid repeating words or characters more than three times (e.g., no "aaaa" or "yeah yeah yeah yeah") in transcripts to prevent Speech service drops.

3. Annotation Taxonomy (Tiers 1-3)

Annotators must apply a hierarchical tag to every identified event. Binary "Violence/Non-Violence" labels are prohibited.

Temporal Granularity

Weak Labels: Tag presence of an event within a 30-120s raw clip.

Strong Labels: Define precise onset () and offset (). Analyze in 3.0s windows with hop length of 1s.

Ambiguous Data: Cull data from the ends of manually labeled events to the 10 dB downpoints to ensure high-energy training samples.

To ensure the model discriminates between true violence and high-energy "safe" events, annotators must include a control group of samples that mimic the acoustic profile of violence without the intent.

Technical Specification for the Audio Violence Dataset Project: Professional Standards for Tagging, Metadata, and Signal Architecture

The proliferation of high-density acoustic sensors and the increasing sophistication of deep learning architectures have transformed the landscape of automated violence detection. Within the framework of the Datahack nonprofit and its flagship DataForBetter program, the Audio Violence Dataset Project (AVDP) serves as a specialized endeavor to create a robust, ethical, and technically sound corpus for learning from audio recordings. This project is specifically designed to support the She-Proves initiative, which focuses on mobile safety frameworks for survivors of domestic violence, and The Elephant in the Room initiative, which addresses cultural intelligence and privacy-centric surveillance on edge hardware.

This document delineates the standards for the acquisition of scripted violence data using volunteer actors and domain-expert scripts, as well as the preprocessing and tagging protocols required for machine learning applications.

Fundamental Principles of Datahack and the DataForBetter Framework

The Datahack nonprofit is an Israeli organization dedicated to advancing the fields of data science and machine learning while harnessing these technologies for social good. The AVDP is a collaborative effort involving The Rackman Center at Bar-Ilan University, providing legal expertise on violence typology, and the Michal Sela Forum, which fosters technological solutions to combat domestic femicide.1

The She-Proves initiative, led by Ivgeni Kucherov, focuses on identifying patterns of domestic coercion and verbal aggression to support legal reform and victim protection.1 Simultaneously, The Elephant in the Room (represented by Mor Herman and Uri) addresses the hardware-side constraints of large-scale data collection. The AVDP bridges these goals by creating a dataset that is "sub-optimal by design," reflecting the messy reality of domestic crises captured on consumer-grade hardware.

Technical Signal Specifications and Edge Device Standards

Unlike standard laboratory corpora, the AVDP prioritizes "Environmental Realism." The reliability of a machine learning model in a safety context is tethered to its ability to generalize across varying microphone qualities and acoustic environments.

Mandatory Multi-Sensor Device Array

To ensure model robustness, every scripted scene must be recorded simultaneously on the following devices:

iOS and Android Handsets: Representing the primary deployment environment for the She-Proves mobile application.

Raspberry Pi with a Budget Microphone: Representing the low-cost hardware configuration for The Elephant in the Room.

High-Fidelity Reference Device: Used to capture a clean signal for baseline comparison and ground-truth verification.

Recording Philosophy: Real-World Constraints

Sub-optimal Placement: Devices should be placed as they would be in an actual domestic dispute (e.g., quickly set on a kitchen counter or a living room shelf).

Dynamic Orientation: Actors may move throughout the room during the performance to simulate varying distances and orientations relative to the sensors, strictly according to script instructions.

Acoustic Hygiene: Despite the focus on noise, at least 0.5s of "silence padding" must be maintained before and after speech segments to allow models to capture the temporal envelope and background noise floor.2

Granular Annotation Taxonomy and Labeling Protocols

The AVDP utilizes a hierarchical tagging schema that captures the nuances of human aggression as defined by social work field experts.

The taxonomy distinguishes between "Situational Couple Violence" (episodic aggression) and "Intimate Terrorism" (chronic coercion) to provide critical context for the She-Proves support extraction modules.20

Tagging Methodology and Quality Assurance

The creation of "strong labels" for training Streaming Audio Transformers (SAT) follows a rigorous six-point methodology:

Temporal Granularity: Annotators must provide precise onset () and offset () for every event. Segments are analyzed in 2.0s windows with a 50% hop length.5

Inter-Annotator Agreement (IAA): A minimum of 20% of the dataset must undergo second-pass review. Target Cohen’s Kappa () is  for physical events and  for verbal aggression.22

Multi-pass Isolation: Use a layered approach where primary speakers and distinct violent events are isolated first, followed by a second pass to identify overlapping environmental sounds.16

Culling Ambiguous Data: To ensure clear training signals, remove data from the ends of manually labeled events to the 10 dB downpoints.10

Conflict Resolution: Any segment with a disagreement in Tier 2 labels is escalated to a senior social worker or domestic violence expert for a final verdict.24

Confidence Scoring: Every label includes an annotator-reported confidence float (0.0 to 1.0). Segments scoring below 0.6 are automatically flagged for review.

Accessible Annotation Workflow

To facilitate tagging by a non-technical audience (e.g., social workers and students), the AVDP employs a "Sheet-to-JSON" pipeline.

Primary Tool (Google Sheets): Field workers enter word-level and segment-level metadata into a structured Google Sheet. This interface minimizes the technical barrier and ensures high compatibility.25

Automated Conversion: A backend script utilizes a Large Language Model (LLM) to map the script instructions and the CSV data from the sheet into the finalized JSON schema required for the AI pipeline.

Verification: The LLM pass acts as a secondary check, flagging entries where the "Emotion" or "Intensity" tags contradict the professional script intent.26

Administrative & Metadata Architecture

Each audio recording requires a corresponding metadata file (in JSON or CSV format) to support "Acoustic Intelligence"—the ability for machines to associate sounds with physical events.23

Preprocessing Pipelines and Signal Hygiene

Before ingestion, data undergoes a rigorous standardization pipeline:

Resampling: All edge device audio is converted to  Hz for model ingestion.27

Amplitude Normalization: Signals are scaled to -1.0 dBFS to prevent amplitude bias during training.27

Spectral Filtering: Low-pass filtering is applied to remove high-frequency noise from budget sensors that does not contribute to event recognition.27

Selective Denoising: Spectral subtraction is used to remove electrical hum from Raspberry Pi setups; however, the original "dirty" audio must be preserved to allow for training on real-world noise distributions.27

Future Scalability: Multi-Modal Fusion

The ultimate goal of the AVDP is to enable real-time hazard inference. Future iterations will support:

Multi-Modal Alignment: Aligning audio predictions with visual cues using late fusion (weighted sum of classification scores).30

Online Risk Verdicts: Systems will provide binary (YES/NO) life-threatening risk decisions every two seconds, along with a brief justification, emphasizing the accountability required for legal and support frameworks.16
